Main project

TITAN

TITAN is WE Bots' autonomous humanoid robot platform. Started in 2020, it is the club's long-term technical project for learning, building, testing, and working toward future humanoid robotics competition.

What is TITAN?

A humanoid robotics platform

TITAN brings together mechanical design, manufacturing, electronics, software, controls, and integration. The project gives members a real system to work on instead of isolated practice problems.

The long-term goal is to develop a robot capable of performing athletic-style humanoid tasks and to prepare the club for future participation in FIRA HuroCup.

Current focus

Current efforts are to complete the robot's upper body design, as the lower body was completed during the 2025/26 Winter Semester.

Project areas

What members can work on

Structure & Motion

Frames, joints, linkages, actuators, gearboxes, CAD, manufacturing, and physical robot assembly.

Power & Electronics

Wiring, batteries, motor drivers, voltage regulation, sensors, embedded devices, and safe hardware integration.

Controls & Software

Control loops, simulations, robot communication, sensor processing, testing tools, and autonomous behaviors.

Roadmap

Road to HuroCup

We are not treating competition as a one-off event. The goal is to build a sustainable platform, documentation, and member knowledge that future WE Bots teams can continue improving.

1

Develop and test reliable humanoid hardware.

2

Build repeatable controls, testing, and documentation workflows.

3

Prepare for future FIRA HuroCup-style events and public demonstrations.
